Arnie's Pattern Cheat Sheet · 2-Min Futures

6 Patterns.
That's All You Need.

The only candle patterns worth memorizing for scalping NQ, RTY, and M2K — high frequency, real edge, no noise.

The rule: Most candlestick books teach you 40+ patterns. 90% of them are noise or appear so rarely that knowing them is useless. These 6 fire constantly on a 2-min chart and — when traded with context — give you a real edge. Master these, ignore the rest.

PATTERN 01

The Pin Bar

Hammer (bottom) · Shooting Star (top) — the rejection candle
Hit rate: ~70% at strong levels
→ Hammer · Bullish reversal at support
→ Shooting Star · Bearish reversal at resistance

What you're looking at

A candle where the body is small and sits in the top or bottom third, with a wick at least 2× the size of the body on the opposite end. It doesn't matter if the body is green or red — what matters is where it closes relative to the wick.

Psychology — why it works

Price tried to extend the move. Someone — usually big money — stepped in hard and rejected it back to where it started. The long wick is a footprint of that fight, and the small body shows who won. On a 2-min chart this often signals a 30–60 minute swing, sometimes longer.

Trade plan

Entry
Buy stop 1–2 ticks above hammer high
(or sell stop below shooting star low)
Stop
1–2 ticks below hammer low
(or above shooting star high)
Target
Min 1.5:1 R:R
Or next 5-min S/R level

Confluence — only trade pin bars with at least 2 of these

  • At a clear support / resistance level you can see on the 5-min or 15-min
  • At a key moving average (VWAP, 20 EMA, 200 EMA)
  • At a Fibonacci level (50%, 61.8%, 78.6%)
  • After an extended move (not in chop)
  • Volume spike on the pin bar itself
PATTERN 02

Engulfing

Two-candle complete sentiment flip
Hit rate: ~72% at levels with volume
→ Bullish Engulfing
→ Bearish Engulfing

What you're looking at

Two candles where the second candle's body completely covers the first candle's body. The second one opens beyond the prior close and closes beyond the prior open — flipping sentiment in one move. Wicks don't matter, only the bodies.

Psychology — why it works

The first candle represents one side's last stand. The engulfing candle is the other side overpowering them completely, in a single 2-minute window. It's the cleanest possible signal of a sentiment shift — buyers (or sellers) suddenly arrived in size and didn't care about the prior move.

Trade plan

Entry
Market at close of engulfing candle (aggressive) OR break of its high/low (safer)
Stop
Opposite end of engulfing candle (above the wick high or below the wick low)
Target
1.5–2× the engulfing candle's own range OR next structure level

Confluence — only trade engulfings with these

  • At a clear S/R level (most important)
  • Volume on the engulfing candle is noticeably larger than the prior 5–10 candles
  • After a clear preceding move — not in chop
  • Higher timeframe (15-min) trend agrees with the engulf direction
  • The engulfing body is at least 1.5× the prior body — bigger is better
PATTERN 03

Morning Star / Evening Star

Three-candle reversal — the textbook bottom & top
Hit rate: ~75% at strong levels w/ confluence
→ Morning Star · Bullish reversal at bottom
→ Evening Star · Bearish reversal at top

What you're looking at

Three candles telling a complete reversal story: (1) a large candle continuing the prior trend, (2) a small-body candle (the "star") showing exhaustion, and (3) a large opposite-direction candle that closes past the midpoint of candle 1's body. The deeper candle 3 penetrates candle 1, the stronger the signal.

Psychology — why it works

This is the only pattern in the set that shows the full sentiment shift across three time periods: dominance → exhaustion → reversal. That extra step of confirmation is why it has a slightly higher hit rate than engulfing — but it's also slower (you have to wait for candle 3 to complete), so you give up some R:R for that reliability.

Trade plan

Entry
Market at close of candle 3
OR break of candle 3 high (conservative)
Stop
Below the low of candle 2 (the star)
Tightest stop in the set
Target
Min 1.5:1 R:R
Previous swing high / next structure

Confluence — when a morning star actually works

  • Candle 3 closes more than 50% into candle 1 body — the deeper, the better
  • At a clear support level (or resistance for evening star)
  • Volume expansion on candle 3 — without it, the move lacks conviction
  • After an extended decline (4+ red candles) — not in chop
  • If candle 2 is a doji, the signal is stronger (called a "Morning Doji Star")
  • Higher timeframe doesn't disagree
PATTERN 04

Inside Bar

Compression before the breakout
Hit rate: ~65% trend-aligned breakouts

What you're looking at

A "mother" candle followed by a smaller candle whose entire range — high AND low — is completely inside the mother's range. The market paused. Volatility compressed. A move is coming.

Psychology — why it works

After a big candle, the market takes a breath. Buyers and sellers reach a temporary truce inside the prior range. But compression is a spring being loaded — eventually one side breaks the deadlock and the move resumes. In trends, the break usually goes in the trend's direction.

Trade plan

Entry
Buy stop above mother high (long) OR sell stop below mother low (short)
Stop
Opposite side of mother bar (or below inside bar for tighter stop)
Target
Project the mother bar's range from breakout point
1.5–2× usually clean

Confluence — these turn inside bars into edge

  • Trade with the trend — the single biggest factor; counter-trend inside bars fail often
  • Inside bar at a known S/R level (compressing AT a level = explosive breakout potential)
  • Multiple inside bars in a row (nested compression = bigger move when it pops)
  • Inside bar after a strong trending move on 5-min chart
  • Mother bar was a momentum candle (large body, small wicks)
PATTERN 05

Marubozu

No wicks. Pure conviction. The momentum signal.
Hit rate: ~68% as trend continuation
→ Bullish Marubozu
→ Bearish Marubozu

What you're looking at

A candle with a tall body and little to no wicks on either end. Opens at one extreme, closes at the other. Nobody pushed back. Pure one-sided conviction over the entire 2 minutes.

Psychology — why it works

When buyers (or sellers) own a candle bell-to-bell with no pushback, momentum almost always carries into the next few candles. This is the "the freight train is moving" pattern. Don't fight it — ride it.

Trade plan

Entry
Aggressive: market at close of marubozu
Conservative: pullback to its midpoint
Stop
Opposite end of the marubozu (stops are wider — size down accordingly)
Target
1× marubozu range minimum
Trail behind structure for runners

Confluence — when to trust a marubozu

  • It's in the direction of the higher-timeframe trend
  • It's a breakout candle — clearing a key level you've been watching
  • Volume on the marubozu is well above average
  • It comes after consolidation (compression → release = high probability move)
  • Caution: a marubozu at the END of an extended move can be exhaustion. Don't chase parabolic marubozus.
PATTERN 06

Doji

The indecision candle — a warning, not a signal
Hit rate: ~65% at extremes only

What you're looking at

A candle where open and close are essentially equal — the body is just a horizontal line. Wicks can be long on top, bottom, or both. It means buyers and sellers were perfectly balanced over the candle.

Psychology — why it works (and when it doesn't)

A doji is indecision frozen in time. In the middle of chop, it means nothing — there are hundreds of dojis on every 2-min chart all day. But at the end of an extended trend or right at a key level, a doji is a warning: the trend has lost its conviction.

Key rule: never trade the doji itself

Dojis are signals to watch, not enter. You wait for the NEXT candle to break the doji's high or low — that's your trigger. A doji that gets engulfed = high-probability reversal. A doji that gets ignored = no trade.

Trade plan

Entry
Break of doji's low (short) or high (long) on the NEXT candle
Stop
Opposite end of doji's wick
(beyond the high if shorting, below low if buying)
Target
1.5–2× the doji's wick-to-wick range
Or nearest structure

Confluence — when a doji actually matters

  • After a clear extended move — 4+ candles in one direction. Not in chop.
  • At a major S/R level, prior swing, or round number
  • At VWAP or a key MA on the 5/15-min
  • Long upper wick + at resistance = especially bearish (rejection doji)
  • Volume spike on the doji = real fight happened — credible signal

The one rule that matters

The pattern is never the edge. The context is the edge. A pin bar in chop is noise. A pin bar at the 200 EMA with volume after a 10-bar push is a layup. Same shape. Different trade.
